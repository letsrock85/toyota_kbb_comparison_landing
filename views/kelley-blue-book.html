Driving Dynamics
This Rating evaluates the quality and personality of a vehicle's engine, drivetrain, suspension, brakes and steering - and what it's like to drive. Relevance to a vehicle's purpose matters most - a sports car will be judged first on its handling and responsiveness, with ride quality being a secondary (but still necessary) consideration. Likewise, a good luxury sedan should be easy to drive and treat its occupants like royalty, but if it also handles well, that will be reflected in the Ratings too. Even ease-of-parking gets taken into account.

& Comfort
Here, we assess what a car is like to live in. Things like seat comfort, headroom, legroom, rear-seat room, infotainment systems, placement of controls, materials and touch surfaces, cabin quietness, outward vision, cargo area(s), ease of ingress/egress, cubbies, cup holders, and more, all get assessed to come up with our Comfort & Convenience Rating.

Interior & Exterior
But is it art? We use this Rating to assess how well the manufacturer was able to create exterior and interior shapes and surfaces that fulfill a vehicle's mission as a practical road-going machine, while at the same time managing to do so in a way that could appeal to a buyer's aesthetic and emotional sides.
Value
This Rating includes everything that makes a vehicle both a good deal and a smart deal. We consider everything from the features-per-dollar relative to a vehicle's competitors to the length of the warranty. Additionally, we take into account a vehicle's quality and reliability, as well as Kelley Blue Book's own 5-Year Cost to Own, Fair Purchase Price, and residual value data.
